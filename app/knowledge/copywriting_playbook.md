# Copywriter Agent Doctrine — Offers & Direct-Response Ad Copy

The combined playbook of Hormozi's *$100M Offers* + *$100M Leads* and Sabri Suby's *Sell Like Crazy*, distilled for an autonomous agent that writes paid image-ad creative (hook + headline + subhead + CTA) and builds offers. Two engines: **Hormozi builds the offer and fills the funnel; Sabri writes the copy that sells it.** Both run on the same value logic and a research-first, "what you say > how you say it" discipline.

> **Order of leverage on success: Market > Offer > Persuasion.** A great market with a mediocre offer beats a great offer in a dying market. Fix in that order. And: *"A strong offer succeeds in spite of weak copy, but strong copy won't overcome a weak offer."* Get the offer and the research right first — the writing is downstream of both.

---

## 1. The Value Equation (the spine of everything)

Use it to score any offer, lead magnet, headline, or ad angle.

```
                Dream Outcome  ×  Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
   Value  =  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 Time Delay  ×  Effort & Sacrifice
```

- **Maximize the top two, minimize (drive toward zero) the bottom two.** "Anything ÷ near-zero ≈ infinite value." Beginners obsess over bigger claims (the top); the best businesses make the bottom near-zero (immediate, effortless).
- Maps to the prospect's **4 questions**: *What will I get? How do I know it'll work? How long will it take? What's required of me?*
- **Perception is reality** — value only lands once they *perceive* higher likelihood, less time, less effort.
- **Logical problems are mostly already solved; what's left is psychological.** Reach for perception levers, not just "faster/cheaper."

**Per-variable tactics:**
1. **Dream Outcome (↑):** sell the feeling/status, not the mechanism. Frame benefits as **status gained in the eyes of others**. When two products satisfy the same desire, the dream cancels out and the other three variables decide the price.
2. **Perceived Likelihood (↑):** "people pay for certainty." Raise with proof, testimonials, track record, guarantees, what you include/exclude.
3. **Time Delay (↓):** they buy the long-term dream but *stay* for short-term wins — engineer **fast wins** near purchase. **"Fast beats free."**
4. **Effort & Sacrifice (↓):** cut tangible + intangible costs. Why done-for-you > do-it-yourself; why supplements outsell gyms (same outcome, less effort).

> Scoring shortcut: rate any offer 0/1 on each variable. Meditation (low) vs. Xanax (high) → same dream outcome, but Xanax wins on likelihood/time/effort → billion-dollar product.

## 2. Market Selection — the Starving Crowd

"Demand > everything." We **channel** demand, we don't create it.

**4 indicators of a great market:**
1. **Massive pain** — "the pain is the pitch." Pain ∝ price you can charge.
2. **Purchasing power** — they can afford it.
3. **Easy to target** — gathered in lists/groups/channels.
4. **Growing** — a tailwind, not a headwind.

Three eternal markets: **Health, Wealth, Relationships.** Find a growing, affordable, easy-to-reach subgroup inside one.

**Niche down — "the riches are in the niches."** The same product sells for far more as you niche:
> Time Management → $19 · …for Sales Pros → $99 · …for Outbound B2B Sales → $499 · …for Outbound B2B Power-Tools Sales Reps → $1,997.

Pick one and commit; don't niche-hop. Under ~$10M/yr, niching makes more money; broaden later by TAM.

**The Larger Market Formula (who you're actually talking to) — Sabri:**
- **3% — Buying now** (in-market, comparing)
- **17% — Gathering information**
- **20% — Problem-aware** (know the problem, not yet looking)
- **60% — Not problem-aware**

Don't fight over the top 3%; value-first marketing captures the 97% everyone ignores. **Match message temperature to traffic: Tinder (cold) → Second Date (warm) → Netflix-and-chill (hot).**

**Your Power 4%:** the top 20% of your top 20% (~4% of customers) drive ~64% of revenue. Bias offer + messaging toward attracting more of them.

## 3. Pricing — charge what it's worth

- People buy to get a **deal** (perceived value > price). Below that crossover = the **Point of Cancellation**.
- Don't compete on price (race to the bottom; "you can only go down to $0, but up is infinite"). Raise value first, then price → sell "money at a discount."
- **Virtuous cycle of price:** higher price → clients more invested → better results → higher perceived value → attracts the best, cheapest-to-serve clients → fatter margins.
- "Charge as high a price as you can say out loud without cracking a smile." "Those who pay the most, pay the most attention." Price so it *stings* a little.
- 99% of businesses should **raise** prices to grow. "Profit is oxygen."

## 4. Dream-Buyer Research — the Halo Strategy (Sabri; do this BEFORE writing a word)

> "Enter the conversation already taking place in the customer's mind." The game is won here. Spend days, not minutes. Redo every quarter.

**Goal:** know the prospect so intimately you can articulate their problem **better than they can themselves** — then they assume you have the solution.

**The Halo research doc** — three ranked rows + a glossary:

| | Most common | 2nd | 3rd | Importance (1–10) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Hopes & Dreams** | | | | |
| **Pains & Fears** | | | | |
| **Barriers & Uncertainties** | | | | |

Plus a **Glossary of Verbiage / Jargon / Niche Terms** — the exact words they use, pasted straight into copy so it reads like "one of us" wrote it.

**Forum foraging (be a fly on the wall, read don't post):** Reddit/subreddits, Quora, Facebook Groups/Pages (sort by most-engaged), YouTube comments, **Amazon/Google reviews — 1★, 3★, AND 5★ — of competitors**, niche blogs/forums, LinkedIn groups. Tools: **Google/Bing auto-suggest**, **AnswerThePublic.com** (who/what/when/where/why/which/can/are/how wheels), "Discussions" Chrome extensions. **Find the gap** — the recurring complaint/unmet need = "your winning ticket."

**The 9 questions that define the Dream Buyer:**
1. Where do they congregate?
2. Where do they get information?
3. Biggest frustrations & challenges?
4. Hopes, dreams, desires? (the Promised Land you'll sell)
5. Biggest fears? (fear of loss > desire for gain)
6. Preferred communication form?
7. Exact phrases/vernacular they use?
8. A day in their life, hour by hour?
9. What makes them happy?

→ Synthesize into **one paragraph** describing a single named avatar. Speak to one person, not a demographic.

## 5. The 8 Non-Negotiable Copy Principles (Sabri)

1. **Sell what people already want to buy.** Channel desire, don't create it. Write the sales message *before* building the product.
2. **Enter the conversation already in their mind.** No introductions, no company talk — walk straight into the problem they're obsessing over.
3. **It's about THEM, never you. 80% about the prospect, ≤20% about you/delivery.** "We/I/our team/17 years in business" is the #1 sin.
4. **What to say > how to say it.** Won in research, not word-finessing. Decide *what* first, polish *how* last.
5. **Benefits sell, features tell.** Sell the end destination (six-pack abs, dress sizes), never the work (counting macros, 6am HIIT). Chain every feature to the emotional outcome.
6. **Specificity is everything — "under the fingernail" copy.** "lose weight" is dead; "a weird 2-minute exercise 45 minutes before bed" lands. The more advertised-to the market, the deeper the specificity.
7. **Write at a 6th-grade level.** Short words, sentences, paragraphs. Run through Hemingway; kill every red/yellow snag. *Consumption precedes conversion.*
8. **Value first; assume deep skepticism.** Make the ad/funnel genuinely helpful. Address objections *before* they arise; sell against everything they've already tried and failed with.

## 6. Build the Offer

### 6a. The Grand Slam / Godfather Offer

So good the choice becomes **your product vs. nothing** (a category of one) — "only a lunatic would refuse." Combines: attractive promotion + unmatchable value + premium price + an unbeatable guarantee + payment terms that get you paid to acquire customers.

**Hormozi's 5-step build:**
1. **Identify the Dream Outcome.** Sell the vacation, not the plane flight. ("Lose 20 lbs in 6 weeks.")
2. **List every problem.** Walk the moment immediately before and after using the product; write each obstacle. Aim for **30–60+ problems**. Each maps to a value-equation fear (not worth it / won't work for me / too hard / takes too long).
3. **Turn each problem into a solution:** "How to [reverse the problem]."
4. **Pick delivery vehicles** via the **Delivery Cube**: level of personal attention (1-on-1 / group / one-to-many) · effort expected (DIY / DWY / DFY) · live medium · recording format · speed & convenience · the **10×-to-1/10th test** ("what would I give if paid 10× / 1/10th?"). Build once, sell for years.
5. **Trim & Stack.**
   - **Trim:** cut high-cost/low-value first, then low-cost/low-value. Keep low-cost/high-value and high-cost/high-value. Highest leverage = **one-to-many**.
   - **Stack:** bundle into one deliverable. Present each as *Problem → solution wording → sexier bundle name*, each with a $ value, totaling far above price (e.g. $4,351 of value for $599).
   - Mantra: **"Create flow, monetize flow, then add friction."**
   - **A single offer is worth less than the same offer split into parts and stacked as bonuses.**

**Sabri's Godfather Offer — 7 parts:**
1. **Rationale** — a believable *reason why* you're being this generous (introductory offer, efficient model, shared enemy). Without a credible why, generosity reads as a scam.
2. **Build value** — anchor against the normal/full price with forensic detail + proof (**show, don't tell**). Then **"reduce it to the ridiculous"**: break the price into a tiny daily figure ("$1.33/day — less than a cup of coffee").
3. **Pricing** — lead with a low front-end price to maximize buyers, then 1–2 upsells. Don't offer too many front-end choices (every decision kills the sale).
4. **Payment options** — break a high price into 3–4 payments. "Today" money beats "tomorrow" money.
5. **Premiums/bonuses** — relevant free gifts can double/triple response. Sweet spot: **3–4 bonuses.**
6. **Power Guarantee** — specific, measurable, named, performance-tied (below).
7. **Scarcity** — genuine only.

Then **anticipate the top 3 objections** and bake the answers into the offer/copy. Ask: "What can I add to make this even more compelling?"

**Detail Sheet (features → benefits):** two columns; chain each feature outward — "so what does that mean for them?" — until you hit the deepest desire.
> latex foam → moulds to body → undisturbed sleep → wake refreshed → perform at work → promotion → more money → bigger house for the family.

**Draft outrageous, then dial back** to something deliverable and lawsuit-proof but still jaw-dropping. Model: Casper "120-night trial"; Enso "your home in 30 weeks or $5,000 cash"; King Kong "Guaranteed Google rankings in 90 days or we work for free." **Avoid weak generic claims:** "great customer service," "outstanding quality," "trusted experts," "X years in business."

### 6b. Enhancers — Scarcity · Urgency · Bonuses · Guarantees

> "Desire comes from not getting what you want." **The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make.**

**Scarcity (limit quantity)** — 3 types: limited seats/units, limited bonuses, never-available-again. Physical: **always sell out and announce it.** Services: Total Business Cap / Growth-Rate Cap (per week) / Cohort Cap (per class). Honest scarcity = advertise your real capacity.

**Urgency (limit time)** — 4 ethical types: **cohort rolling** ("next group kicks off Monday"), **rolling seasonal** (rename the promo each season), **pricing/bonus-based** (the *deal* expires — lets year-round sellers use urgency), **exploding opportunity** (the chance itself decays). 50–60% of launch sales come in the last 4 hours.

**Bonuses** — add value instead of discounting. In 1-on-1, ask for the sale first, then reveal bonuses (or add one per objection). Rules: benefit-named, tied to their obstacle, proven, paint the after-picture, **ascribe a $ price**, tools/checklists > more training, value should **eclipse** the core offer, can stack with scarcity/urgency.

**Guarantees (reverse the risk)** — #1 objection is risk. A bold guarantee can 2–4× conversion, called on <5% of the time. Structure: **"If you don't get [X] in [Y], we will [Z]"** (the "or what" is essential). 4 types:
- **Unconditional** (pay first, try it; strongest, most refunds) — "no questions asked."
- **Conditional** ("better than money back," tied to required success-actions) — service guarantee (work free until X — Hormozi's favorite), credit-based, delayed second payment, "we pay your ad spend until your first sale."
- **Anti-guarantee** ("all sales final") — for consumables/exposed IP; needs a reason-why.
- **Implied** (performance / revshare / profit-share / ratchets) — "if I don't perform, I don't get paid."

Reverse-engineer: *what are they most afraid of? Turn that fear into the guarantee.* A guarantee amplifies a good offer; it can't save a bad one.

**Power Guarantee — 7 build steps (Sabri):** study competitors' guarantees → laser on your real strength → be specific & measurable (number/outcome/timeframe, not "satisfaction") → choose a payback low-cost to you / high perceived value → test (longer usually wins) → put it front-and-center everywhere → **give it a name.** Named examples: "100% Money-Back Triple-Protection Guarantee," "Better Than Risk-Free," "110% Call-Me-Crazy Guarantee," Domino's "30 minutes or it's free."

### 6c. Name it — the MAGIC formula

Use **3–5**, any order, short and punchy:
- **M — Magnetic reason why** (Free, 88% off, Spring, Grand Opening, Anniversary)
- **A — Announce the avatar** (hyper-specific: "Towson MD Dentists," "Salon Owners")
- **G — Give a goal** (dream outcome: "Pain Free," "Double Your Profit," "First Client")
- **I — Indicate an interval** (4-Hour, 21-Day, 6-Week). ⚠ Ad-compliance: don't pair a *quantifiable* income/weight claim with a duration (reads as a guarantee).
- **C — Container word** (Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Intensive, Accelerator, Masterclass, System)

Rhyme ("Six-Pack Fast Track") or alliterate ("Make Money Masterclass"). Template: `[Magnetic reason why] + [Avatar] + [Goal] + [Time interval] + [Container word]` → e.g. "Free 6-Week Lean-By-Summer Challenge."

**When an offer fatigues, change the wrapper, not the offer** — order: creative → body copy → headline/name → duration → enhancer → monetization (last resort).

## 7. The Ad Itself — "Sell the Click"

The ad's only job is to provoke primal desire and earn the click — **not to sell the product** (they usually can't buy from the ad anyway). The market is a **raging river** of distracted, sleepwalking prospects; the ad competes with everything in their feed, not the competitor down the street.

**The ad needs (Sabri):**
- A **big, specific benefit** that calls out the audience (the more advertised-to, the more specific).
- **Intrigue** — enough curiosity they must click.
- A **trigger emotion**: curiosity (strongest), shock/pattern-interrupt, implied/direct benefit, vanity, fear, self-interest. Layer curiosity *behind* the benefit so blind curiosity doesn't pull the wrong people.

**The ad scaffold (Hormozi): Call Out + Value + CTA.**

**Callout (first 5 seconds — 20× leverage):**
- **Label:** "[Local area] [type of person]" ("Phoenix homeowners…")
- **Yes-Question:** "Do you [specific pain]?"
- **If-Then:** "If you [situation], we can [benefit]…"
- **Ridiculous Result:** "This [person] [outcome] and [unexpected twist]."
- Nonverbal callouts: contrast, likeness ("quack like a duck"), the scene.

**Value — the What-Who-When framework (8 elements):** show the **4 value drivers AND their opposites** — Dream↔Nightmare, Likelihood↔Risk, Speed↔Delay, Ease↔Effort — from the viewpoint of a **status-giver** (spouse / kids / boss / rivals), across **When** (past / present / future).

**CTA:** spell out exactly what to do + a reason to act now. "Don't be clever, be clear."

## 8. Headlines (spend ~80% of your time here)

> 5× more people read the headline than the body. Same content, retitled, sold 2.3M copies. **Write 20+, trim to the best ~6, build "Franken-headlines"** by splicing winning elements. Channel/Facebook data: ~12–18 words / 80–95 chars performs well.

**The 5 headline essentials:**
1. **Grab by the throat.** Trigger words: *Exposed! Revealed! Confession! Secret! Shocking! Warning! Banned!* Most "grabbers" are 3–4 words.
2. **Use numbers.** Gives the brain a tangible object. ("The 7 things no financial planner will tell you.")
3. **Load with curiosity.** Power words (*Must, Alarming, Won't Tell You, Secret*) + a twist/qualifier; address the biggest objection right in the headline ("…even if you're a complete beginner").
4. **WIIFM** — a big specific benefit, written about *them*. Promise a benefit, don't be cute or negative.
5. **Be hyper-specific.** Not "grow your business" but "150–250 qualified leads/month on autopilot."

> Escalation: "7 Things to Know About Financial Planning" → "7 **Alarming** Things You **Must** Know **Before Hiring** a Financial Planner (**#3 Will Blow Your Mind!**)"
> Format model: *National Enquirer* / supermarket-tabloid covers + TMZ/NYT/LADbible. Boring is the only unforgivable sin. Never write a headline the body can't back up.

**Headline / lead-magnet title formulas (swipe & deploy):**
1. `X ways to achieve [Desirable Thing] without doing [Undesirable Thing]` → "6 Ways to Get Washboard Abs Without Doing a Single Sit-Up"
2. `[Do Difficult Thing] in [Specific Period] Even if [Shortcoming]` → "Pay Off Your Mortgage in 7 Years Even if You Have a Modest Income"
3. `Achieve [Desirable Thing] like [An Expert] Even Without [Something Expected]` → "Paint Masterpieces Like Picasso Without Ever Having a Single Lesson"
4. `How to Eliminate [Biggest Problem] without doing [The Thing They Hate] Within [Timeframe]` → "How to Eliminate Your Muffin Top Without Giving Up the Foods You Love — Within 21 Days"
5. `Shocking reporting reveals the [N] things you must know about [X].`
6. `The [N] things that everybody ought to know about [X].`
7. `[N] [Power Word] Things You Must Know Before [Action] (#3 Will Blow Your Mind!)`

**Opt-in headline formulas:**
- `Finally! How to Get [DESIRED RESULT] Without [THING THEY FEAR MOST] in [TIME FRAME] — Guaranteed.`
- `[PROBLEM]? Get My [SOLUTION] and [RESULT].`

**Case-study lead-magnet headline (Hormozi):**
- `FREE Case Study: How we [specific result with numbers] for a [specific avatar] in [timeframe].` → "How we added 213 members and $112,000 to a small gym in San Diego."

## 9. Fascination Bullets (the curiosity secret weapon)

Small teaser nuggets that intrigue while implying a benefit — driven by **curiosity** ("among the strongest of human incentives"). Each should be intriguing enough to opt in / buy **for that one bullet alone**. **Tease, don't tell.** Most-read, scannable part of a page; any strong one can be split-tested as a headline.

**Fascination formulas (swipe & deploy):**
- **How to [X] without [Y]** → "How to invest in real estate with no money down."
- **You need [X], right? WRONG!** → "Drinking 3 litres of water a day is healthy, right? WRONG!"
- **[N] ways to [Y]** → "5 ways to meet single women in Melbourne."
- **Where to find [Y]** → "Where to find the most ravenous, hyperactive buyers online."
- **How to eliminate [X]** → "How to stop joint pain forever."
- **What you should never [X]** (loss aversion) → "What you should never say to a woman on a first date."
- **Say goodbye to [frustration]** → "Say goodbye to calorie counting and hour-long cardio sessions."
- **The truth about [Y]** → "The dirty truth about fish oil revealed."
- **Have you been [doing X] wrong? (And does it really matter?)** → "Have you been boiling your eggs in the wrong pot?"
- **The little-known secret to [result] that [authority] don't want you to know.**

## 10. The 17-Step Secret Selling System (long-form sales page / VSL / letter)

Long-form beats short-form *when every word earns its place* — buyers read all of it.

1. **Call out your audience** — eyebrow copy above the headline ("Attention property investors who want to become millionaires…").
2. **Demand attention** — a big-promise, tabloid-style headline.
3. **Back up the big promise** — the subheadline supports/expands it and pulls into the body.
4. **Create irresistible intrigue** — fascination bullets (write 10–20, trim to ~6).
5. **Shine a floodlight on the problem** — agitate the pain in vivid "under-the-fingernail" detail until "that's *exactly* how I feel." Name what they've tried and why it failed. (People flee pain harder than they chase pleasure.)
6. **Provide the solution** — your product as the best path; show how it differs from what failed them.
7. **Show your credentials** — results, case studies, prestige clients, customer counts, press, awards.
8. **Detail the benefits** — features→benefits in bullets.
9. **Social proof** — testimonials, video, stats, screenshots, numbers. Show, don't tell.
10. **Make your Godfather Offer** — value-stacked, irresistible.
11. **Add bonuses** — desirable but not essential; 3–4 is the sweet spot.
12. **Stack the value** — total everything; drive perceived value way up.
13. **Reveal the price** — after the value stack, reveal a much lower price; explain *why*; reduce to the ridiculous.
14. **Inject scarcity** — genuine only.
15. **Give a powerful guarantee** — named, specific, risk-reversing.
16. **Call to action** — a **command**, one action; walk them through exactly what to do.
17. **Close with a P.S.** (third most-read element) — restate offer, warn of the cost of inaction, repeat CTA + scarcity. Lead with the negative, end on the dream outcome.

## 11. Lead Magnets & Lead Generation (Hormozi $100M Leads)

**Advertising = "making known."** An **engaged lead** (someone who shows interest) is the true output. Get leads **More, Better, Cheaper, Reliably.**

**Lead magnet = a complete solution to a narrow problem** (cheap/free) that, once solved, **reveals the next problem your core offer solves** (salty pretzels → thirst → drink). "A person who pays with time now is likelier to pay with money later."

**7 steps to a great lead magnet:** (1) pick the problem + who (narrow but meaningful; revealed problem must be solved by your core offer); (2) choose a **type** — *Reveal their problem* (diagnosis/audit), *Sample/trial* (recurring solutions), or *One step of a multi-step process* (complex problems); (3) choose **delivery** — software / information / service / physical (make many versions, rotate); (4) name/test it (headline first); (5) make it easy to consume; (6) make it genuinely good — **"give away the secrets, sell the implementation"**; (7) make it easy to say "I want more" → the CTA.

**CTA = what to do + reasons to act now** ("Don't be clever, be clear"). Reasons-now: real scarcity, urgency, or **any reason after "because."** If free stuff won't move, "your free stuff is too expensive" — cut its time/effort cost.

**The Core Four (the only 4 ways one person advertises):**

| | 1-to-1 (private) | 1-to-many (public) |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm** (gave permission) | #1 Warm Outreach | #2 Post Free Content |
| **Cold** (strangers) | #3 Cold Outreach | #4 Paid Ads |

Do **one**, max it out, then add another. More time than money → content; more money than time → cold outreach / paid ads. Paid ads go **last** (the other three fund them).

**Content Unit = Hook · Retain · Reward.** Hook (get noticed — headlines with ≥2 of Recency / Relevancy / Celebrity / Proximity / Conflict / Unusual / Ongoing). Retain (curiosity via Lists / Steps / Stories). Reward (value per second — "no such thing as too long, only too boring"). Monetize via the **Give:Ask ratio** — **"give until they ask."**

**Scale any method: More → Better → New.** MORE = the **Rule of 100** (100 primary actions/day for 100 days). BETTER = one test/week at the biggest-drop-off constraint. NEW only after both exhausted. **Open to Goal:** commit to the outcome, not a number of attempts.

**Lead Getters (others advertise for you):** Customer Referrals → Employees → Agencies → Affiliates.

## 12. The Magic Lantern — nurture sequence (Sabri)

Converts the 97% who don't buy now. A series of **2–3 value-only videos/emails** (build to 4–5 milestones), **80% value / 20% pitch.** Map a timeline from Before State to Desired Outcome; each piece delivers a *real* result; never leap in one step; always hint at the next ("wait till you see what I've got in 2–3 days"). **End CTA:** "If you liked this and you're committed to getting [X], I've set aside time in my calendar to help you — book a time here."

## 13. Diagnostic Rules of Thumb

- **LTGP : CAC** (Lifetime Gross Profit ÷ Cost to Acquire) — below **3:1** struggling; above 3:1 takes off.
- **CAC vs. 3× industry-avg:** below 3× avg → fix the **business model (LTGP)**; above 3× → fix the **advertising (CAC)**.
- **Sales vs. advertising problem:** if leads have the problem *and* the money but don't buy → **sales** problem, not advertising.
- **Client-Financed Acquisition:** if a customer pays more than it costs to get + fulfill them within 30 days, scale forever; if not, add an upsell.
- **AI usage:** never ask an LLM to "write a high-converting sales page" cold (generic, un-specific). The human supplies the big idea, angle, and under-the-fingernail specificity; AI acts as **copy chief** to assemble and multiply ("give me 5 variations of this *winning* headline for these 4 avatars").

## 14. Quality Checklist (run before declaring done)

- [ ] **80%+** about the prospect, ≤20% about us?
- [ ] **Enters the conversation in their mind** — straight into their problem, no throat-clearing?
- [ ] **Headline grabs by the throat** (specific benefit + intrigue), readable on mobile?
- [ ] Selling **benefits / the Promised Land**, not features/work?
- [ ] **"Under-the-fingernail" specific** — could only this prospect have written it?
- [ ] Would **only a lunatic refuse** the offer? Real scarcity? Named guarantee?
- [ ] **Every claim proven** (numbers, proof, social proof); top 3 objections handled pre-emptively?
- [ ] Sells against **what they've already tried and failed**?
- [ ] **6th-grade** reading level, read-aloud smooth, no walls of text?
- [ ] **CTA commands** one specific action?

**Word/trigger bank:** Exposed, Revealed, Confession, Secret, Shocking, Warning, Banned, Must, Alarming, Free, Finally, Guaranteed, New, Now, Because. **Avoid:** vague claims ("lose weight," "grow your business"), "we/I/our team/years in business," "great service/quality/team," cute/clever lines, anything boring.

## What to Apply When Writing ONE Image Ad

The decision sequence for the Copywriter agent producing a single image-ad creative (hook + headline + subhead + CTA):

1. **Confirm the market & temperature first.** Is the crowd starving (pain / power / targetable / growing)? Which Larger-Market band are we hitting (cold 60–20% vs. hot 3%)? This sets aggression and specificity. *(Value Equation + Starving Crowd + Larger Market Formula)*
2. **Pull the angle from research, not assumption.** Mine the avatar's #1 pain, their exact vernacular, and the recurring "gap." Pick ONE big idea / one promise per ad. *(Halo Strategy — the 9 questions, glossary)*
3. **Lock the dream outcome + its opposite.** Decide the single benefit/Promised Land you sell (status in others' eyes), and the nightmare of *not* acting. Score it on the Value Equation; lean on whichever variable is weakest in the category (usually likelihood/time/effort). *(Value Equation; Detail Sheet features→benefits)*
4. **Write the HOOK (callout, first 5 seconds / top visual line).** Choose one: Label · Yes-Question · If-Then · Ridiculous Result. It must call out the exact avatar and trigger an emotion (curiosity layered behind benefit). *(Hormozi Call-Out + Sabri trigger emotions)*
5. **Write the HEADLINE.** Run a formula (§8). Must hit all 5 essentials: grab + number + curiosity + WIIFM + hyper-specificity. Generate 20+, pick the best, Franken-splice. Never a claim the offer can't back. *(Headline formulas)*
6. **Write the SUBHEAD.** It backs up / expands the big promise and adds the credibility-or-curiosity bridge — often a fascination-bullet formula or the objection-handler ("even if…"). Surface proof or the risk-reversal hint here. *(Fascination formulas + Perceived Likelihood lever)*
7. **Anchor the OFFER element if the ad carries one.** Compress the Grand Slam/Godfather into one line: dream outcome + time + "without [effort]" + the named guarantee + one scarcity/urgency reason. MAGIC-name the offer if shown. *(Grand Slam / Godfather + MAGIC + guarantee + scarcity)*
8. **Write the CTA.** A command, one action, plus a reason to act now (real scarcity / urgency / "because"). "Sell the click," not the product. Clear over clever. *(Hormozi CTA + lead-magnet CTA rules)*
9. **Polish pass.** 80/20 them-vs-us check, 6th-grade readability, read aloud, replace every vague word with a specific one, confirm every claim is provable, ensure the top objection is pre-handled. *(8 Principles + Quality Checklist)*
10. **Produce variations.** Same winning structure, vary the hook/angle per avatar band; build Franken-headlines from winners for split-testing. *(AI copy-chief model + Rule of 100 testing discipline)*
